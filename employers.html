The offer

No employer premium. No new plan. Real retention value.

WorkWell is a direct-pay marketplace. Your workers pay their own monthly fee. You approve distribution and communications. That's the model.

The workers who benefit most are the ones your current plan doesn't reach — part-time, variable-hour, seasonal, or those who opted out because the premium was unaffordable.

  • No employer premium contribution

    Employees pay directly. You are not adding a new benefit line to your budget.

  • No new plan complexity

    WorkWell is not a health insurance plan. It is not an ACA-equivalent or MEC. No new compliance burden from the product itself.

  • Low operational burden

    You approve the population and the communication. WorkWell handles enrollment, activation, support, and reporting.

  • Retention signal, not just a perk

    Workers who have access to care show up. Workers who don't leave for roles that offer something real.

  • Runs through your broker or partner

    WorkWell is distributed through Colonial Life, HUB International, Selerix, and other channel partners — no direct procurement required.

Common questions from employers.

Does offering WorkWell create any ERISA or ACA compliance exposure?

WorkWell is not a health insurance plan. Compliance review is required before launch, and WorkWell works through your Colonial Life or broker partner to ensure required reviews happen before distribution.

We already offer a telehealth vendor. How is this different?

A telehealth vendor is one ingredient. WorkWell combines telemedicine, pharmacy discounts, local care, mental health, and optional financial protection in one direct-pay package — for the workers your current plan doesn't reach at all.

What happens when a worker has a problem?

WorkWell owns the support path for health access issues. Colonial Life or the approved TPA owns supplemental policy questions. Support is routed by issue type — you are not the first call.

Can we pilot with one location or one segment first?

Yes. A single location, division, or workforce segment is the recommended launch pattern — especially for employers launching for the first time.
